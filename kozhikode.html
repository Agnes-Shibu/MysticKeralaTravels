Kozhikode District

Kozhikode, historically known as Calicut, is a city steeped in maritime heritage and cultural significance. As a major trading hub on the Malabar Coast, it played a pivotal role in the spice trade during ancient times. Its historic port of Beypore continues to craft traditional wooden dhows, showcasing centuries-old craftsmanship and seafaring traditions

The city's rich cultural tapestry is evident in its landmarks like the iconic Calicut Beach and the centuries-old Tali Temple. Vibrant markets like Chalai Bazaar offer a sensory feast of spices, textiles, and handicrafts, reflecting the region's vibrant trade and craftsmanship. Kozhikode's bustling streets are alive with color, activity, and the aromas of its legendary cuisine.

Kozhikode's culinary scene is legendary, with its Malabar cuisine earning international acclaim for its bold flavors and intricate blends of spices. Local delicacies like Kozhikode biryani, banana chips, and fresh seafood dishes tantalize the taste buds, offering a culinary journey through Kerala's diverse flavors. Street food stalls and traditional eateries provide authentic dining experiences that capture the essence of Kozhikode's cultural heritage.

Despite its bustling urban atmosphere, Kozhikode offers tranquil escapes amidst its natural beauty. Scenic spots like Kappad Beach, where Vasco da Gama first set foot in India, provide idyllic settings for relaxation and leisure. The nearby Wayanad district offers verdant hills, lush forests, and cascading waterfalls, inviting travelers to explore the region's pristine wilderness.
